### Tutorial Topics and Schedule:

**_Topic 1:Transportation Simulation_**<br>
•	Tutorials: Week 1-4<br>
•	Related Lectures: Lecture 1, Lecture 13-17<br>
•	Learning objective:<br>
&emsp; o Learn the importance of traffic simulation as a way of facilitating the plan, design, and operation in transportation system. <br>
&emsp; o Learn the basic theories and methods under the simulation software.<br>
&emsp; o Learn the steps of building a simulation and analyzing results in SUMO.<br>
&emsp; o Finally, you will be assigned a group project with some small subtasks to apply simulation technology into real-world examples.<br>

**_Topic 2: Transportation Planning_**<br>
•	Tutorials: Week 5-6<br>
•	Related Lectures: Lecture 4-9, Lecture 12<br>
•	Learning objective:<br>
&emsp; o	Understand the principles of effective transportation planning and the relationship between land use and transportation.<br>
&emsp; o	Understand the four-step transportation planning process; Apply the four-step model in practice problems.<br>
{:.success}

**_Topic 3: Traffic Flow and Highway Capacity_**<br>
•	Tutorials: Week 7-10<br>
•	Related Lectures: Lecture 13-18<br>
•	Learning objective:<br>
&emsp; o	Learn the impact of highway system on American cities.<br>
&emsp; o	Understand the basics of traffic flow theory and shockwaves & time-Space Diagrams.<br>
&emsp; o	Know  different types of traffic data and definitions.<br>
&emsp; o	Understand Capacity and LOS for highway segment.<br>
{:.info}

**_Topic 4: Urban Street Design_**<br>
•	Tutorials: Week 11-13<br>
•	Related Lectures: Lecture 19-26<br>
•	Learning objective:<br>
&emsp; o	Understand the vertical and horizontal components of geometric design.<br>
&emsp; o	Understand how to design a basic roadway and its corresponding principles.<br>
&emsp; o	Understand the design characteristics in urban context.<br>
•	Project Deadline: Nature Lodge design project- **Dec. 7th by the end of day.**<br>
{:.info}

### Transportation Courses beyond CE 2710:
At UConn, [the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering](https://cee.engr.uconn.edu/) provides a number of transportation related courses which are built upon the basic contents in CE 2710. If you are interested in transportation and would like to dig into greater depth, check this list (as of August, 2020):

Undergraduate Courses:<br/>
**CE4210. Operations Research in Civil and Environmental Engineering**<br/>
**CE4410. Computer Aided Site Design**<br/>
**CE4900W & CE4920W Civil Engineering Projects I and II**

Mixed Undergraduate/Graduate Courses:<br/>
**CE4710/CE5710. Case Studies in Transportation Engineering**: Analysis of case studies in transportation and urban planning and design. Application of transportation engineering and planning skills. Oral and written group reports, group discussions, individual written papers.<br/>
**CE4720/CE5720. Street and Highway Design**: History of street and highway design; land-use context, street design data collection and analysis, speed, safety and street network characterization; pedestrian and bikers in design, cross-section and alignment design.<br/>
**CE4730/CE5730. Transportation Planning**: Transportation economics, urban transportation planning process, evaluation of transportation improvements, transportation systems management.<br/>
**CE4740/CE5740. Traffic Engineering I**: Traffic flow characteristics; traffic control devices; traffic signs and markings; traffic data collection; traffic signal timing and operation; capacity of streets, intersections, and highways; traffic impact studies; traffic simulation.

Graduate Courses:<br/>
**CE5725. Transportation Safety**<br/>
**CE5735. Public Transportation Systems**<br/>
**CE6725. Statistical and Econometric Methods for Transportation Data Analysis**: Application of various statistical methods for analysis of transportation data, including linear regression, count data models, logistic regression, discrete outcome models, ordered probability models, random parameter models, and duration models among others.<br/>
**CE6730. Travel Demand Forecasting**<br/>
**CE6735. Transportation Network Analysis**<br/>
**CE6740. Traffic Engineering Operations**<br/>
